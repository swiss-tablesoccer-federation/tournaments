# Regulations

## Swiss Tablesoccer League

Version 2.3_2026

*This document was originally drafted in German. The German version is authoritative in both written and spoken form. Versions in other languages have been partly produced using electronic translation.*

# General Rules

## 1. Competence

1.1 The regulations of the Swiss Tablesoccer League, which supplement the statutes and rules of the STF, are drafted by the organising committee of the Swiss Tablesoccer League. The STF board has a right of veto over every amendment recommended by the organising committee. This right of veto ensures that no changes are made to the regulations that do not align with the strategic direction of the STF.

## 2. Licence

2.1 Only clubs that have previously been granted a licence by the Swiss Tablesoccer Federation may register teams for the competition.

2.2 A club may register several teams, which may also play in the same league. If a club registers several teams, the team name must comply with point 3.2.

2.3 Women must decide whether they wish to compete in the open Tablesoccer League or in the Women's Tablesoccer League.

2.4 Players without an annual licence must obtain a day licence for the Tablesoccer League. This single day licence is valid for the entire competition, even if it spans several days.

2.5 Players may only play for one club during the year. This means that players may not compete for different clubs in the Swiss Tablesoccer Cup and the Swiss Tablesoccer League.

2.6 In accordance with ITSF guidelines, a player is not allowed to play the Champions League with one club and, in the same season, participate in a national league with another club.

## 3. Financial obligations

3.1 Financial obligations are governed by the Financial Regulations on Tournaments & Licensing.

## 4. Team name

4.1 The team name must relate to the name of the club that is the licence holder of the team. At most one sponsor may be mentioned in the name (e.g. TFC City Adidas). The team name may not be changed during the season; exceptions are sponsors that are integrated into the team name.

4.2 If a club fields several teams, the teams must be designated with a subordinate identifier. Valid additions are: A, B, C, … or 1, 2, 3, …

## 5. Marketing

5.1 Sponsors and advertising partners for which a licensee claims advertising space in the club's own area on the STF website must be approved by the STF.

5.2 The teams commit to the following obligations:
- Written response to a short interview (3–5 questions) in advance of the Tablesoccer League.
- Participation in a group photo during the competition.

## 6. Clothing

6.1 All players must appear in matching upper clothing. Permitted clothing consists of tracksuit jackets and trousers, jerseys, sports T-shirts, polo shirts, sports shorts and sports shoes. Caps, visors, sweatbands and sports headscarves are also permitted.

6.2 All players must wear shoes with a non-marking sole.

6.3 Jeans and cargo trousers are not allowed. Flip-flops, sandals and non-sporting shoes are likewise not permitted.

## 7. Titles and prizes

7.1 The winning team may call itself "Swiss Champion Tablesoccer League in the year 20xx". The winning team of the Challenge League may call itself "Swiss Champion Tablesoccer Challenge League in the year ...", etc.

7.2 For the winning team of the top leagues there is a challenge trophy which must be returned intact for the following season.

7.3 (In accordance with ITSF.) The winner of the Super League automatically qualifies for that year's European Champions League. If more than 15 teams take part in the Tablesoccer League, the second-placed team is also eligible to play. If more than 39 teams take part in the Tablesoccer League, the third-placed team is also eligible. (Women's league: more than 7 teams -> the second-placed team is eligible. More than 19 teams -> the third-placed team is eligible.)

7.4 If a qualified team does not wish to take part in the Champions League, the STF must be informed as soon as possible. In this case, the next-placed team takes its place.

7.5 No prize money is paid out.

7.6 The STF does not contribute to travel costs or other expenses for teams qualified for the Champions League.

## 8. Claims for damages against the STF

8.1 Claims for damages against the STF, its bodies and the arbitration tribunal on the basis of their actions under the present statutes and other rules and regulations of the STF are excluded, unless a licensee or an athlete proves that the damage was caused intentionally or through gross negligence, that the club or athlete has taken all legal remedies to avert the damage, and that the injured party cannot obtain compensation by other means.

## 9. Severability clause

9.1 The invalidity of individual provisions of these regulations does not result in the invalidity of the regulations as a whole.

# Playing Rules

## 1. Tables and balls

1.1 The following tables are approved for the Tablesoccer League: Garlando, Leonhart and Ullrich in the respective agreed version.

1.2 On each table, the official ball provided by the respective table manufacturer (as defined by the ITSF) is used. The balls used must be in as-new condition.

1.3 The use of additives on the playing surface or on the balls is not permitted.

1.4 The STF arranges balls that can be purchased by teams on the day of play.

## 2. Team registration

2.1 In the Open League, each team registers a minimum of 6 and a maximum of 9 players. The club is responsible for verifying any registration on the STF website.

2.2 Each women's team registers a minimum of 3 and a maximum of 6 players. The club is responsible for verifying the registration on the STF website.

2.3 In justified cases, players may be added after the registration deadline. The decision lies with the organising committee.

## 3. Team no-show

3.1 If fewer than 5 players are available to a team in the Open League on the day of play, this counts as a no-show. With 5 players, the first singles match is recorded as a 5:3; 5:3 defeat. In the Women's League it counts as a no-show with fewer than 3 players.

3.2 If a team is present at the venue but, for whatever reason, does not play, this is treated as a no-show. All results are accordingly cancelled.

3.3 In all such cases, the team in question is not entitled to a refund of the entry fees pursuant to point 2.2 and is no longer eligible to play for the remainder of the Tablesoccer League.

## 4. Permission to play and player pass

4.1 The prerequisite for a player's participation in the competition is that they hold a valid STF annual licence for the year in question, or obtain a day licence for the Tablesoccer League.

4.2 During the current season, a player may only be registered for one team, i.e. transfers during the current season are not permitted.

## 5. League classification and names

5.1 A league consists of 8 or more teams. A new, lower league is only opened from 15 remaining teams onwards (1–15 teams -> 1 league; 16–23 teams -> 2 leagues; 24–31 teams -> 3 leagues; …).

5.2 The first league is named the Super League, the second league the Challenge League and the third league the Promotion League. All further leagues are named, consecutively, 1st, 2nd, 3rd, … Tablesoccer League.

5.3 The classification is based on the previous year's final standings. If several leagues are launched and teams from the top 8 or 16 of the previous year no longer compete, the teams move up into the higher leagues according to the standings. Relegated teams have priority over the highest-placed teams of the league below when moving up. The reference for this is the final standings.

5.4 The women have a league with at least 4 teams. If there are fewer than 4 registrations, the league does not take place in the year concerned.

## 6. Format

### 6.1 Open League format

6.1.1 In leagues with exactly 8 teams, 7 matches are played in the preliminary round using the round-robin system (each team plays each other). In leagues with more than 8 teams (max. 15), seven preliminary rounds are played using the Swiss system. Matches already decided in the preliminary round are played out to the end.

6.1.2 In the event of a tie on team points between two or more teams after the preliminary round, the following count: i) game points won, ii) goals scored, and iii) the head-to-head result. If all these criteria are also tied, lots are drawn.

6.1.3 In leagues with exactly eight teams, the top four enter the semifinals and play for the Swiss Champion title of the respective league (see also Figure 1). The other teams play directly for relegation (relegation matches). In leagues with more than 8 teams (lowest league), the top four enter the semifinals and play for entry into the promotion matches. The other teams play placement matches. All places are played out (for relegation matches and the final ranking for the classification in the following year).

6.1.4 The winners of the semifinals play in the league finals for the title of the respective league; the losers play for third place. Winning teams of the league finals (if not the Super League) are directly promoted to the next-higher league. Losing teams of the league finals (if not the Super League) advance to the promotion/relegation play-offs.

6.1.5 The losing teams of the first round of relegation matches play (if not the lowest league) in league relegation finals for participation in the promotion/relegation play-offs. The losing team is directly relegated to the next-lower league. The winning team advances to the promotion/relegation play-offs.

6.1.6 All knockout rounds are always seeded again according to the preliminary-round standings, with the best-ranked team playing against the worst-ranked one in each case.

6.1.7 In the preliminary round as well as in the knockout phase, the following matches are played: Doubles1 – Doubles2 – Singles1 – Singles2 – Doubles3 – Doubles4. Two sets are played to 5 goals, with a two-goal difference, up to a maximum of 6 goals.

6.1.8 Each team may take 2 timeouts per set.

6.1.9 In the first four matches (D1, D2, S1, S2), six different players must be fielded. If only 5 players are present, match S1 is recorded as a 5:3; 5:3 loss.

6.1.10 In the further two matches (D3, D4), four different players must likewise be fielded.

6.1.11 A player may be fielded in a maximum of 2 matches and may not play more than one singles match.

6.1.12 The team line-ups, including substitutes, must be entered in full before the start of the match and exchanged with the opposing team.

6.1.13 During a match, two substitutions are possible. Only players who have already completed a full match, i.e. only in D3 or D4 (women also S2), may be substituted out. The substitution must take place before the start of a match and must be communicated to the opposing captain. Only players who have not yet played a match may be substituted in. Players who have been substituted out can therefore no longer be substituted in. Likewise, players who have been substituted in cannot be substituted out again.

### 6.2 Women's League format

6.2.1 In the preliminary round as well as in the knockout phase, the following matches are played: Doubles 1 – Singles 1 – Singles 2 – Doubles 2. Two sets are played to 5 goals, with a two-goal difference, up to a maximum of 6 goals.

6.2.2 In the first two matches (D1, S1), three different players must be fielded.

6.2.3 In the further two matches (S2, D2), three different players must likewise be fielded.

6.2.4 Substitutions are only allowed if a player has already completed a full match, i.e. only in S2, D2.

6.2.5 In all other respects, the Women's League format is identical to the Open League.

## 7. Scoring

7.1 Singles and doubles matches are played in 2 sets each, to 5 goals with a two-goal difference up to a maximum of 6 goals. As two more points are still played at 4:4, there is always a winner in every set. One game point is awarded for each set won: two sets won = 2:0 game points; one set won and one set lost = 1:1 game points; two sets lost = 0:2 game points.

7.2 Match won = 2 team points in the preliminary round. Match lost = 0 team points in the preliminary round. Drawn match (6:6 game points) = 1:1 team points in the preliminary round.

7.3 Drawn matches (6:6 game points) in the knockout phase are decided by a deciding doubles.

7.4 Matches not played or not played to the end are recorded as 2:0 game points.

7.5 All match results must be recorded in writing immediately after the end of the match. Entry is made via the official tournament app. Keeping a copy of the results is recommended.

## 8. Serve, choice of table side

8.1 Before each match, and before exchanging the team line-up, the teams perform a coin toss: the winner can decide which side they wish to play on, or on which table the match begins. The loser of the toss has the remaining option. The choice of table side applies to both tables and the entire match. The table is changed after each set. A team always has the first serve per set on its respective home table.

## 9. Deciding doubles

9.1 Before the start of the deciding doubles, both teams nominate two players.

9.2 The deciding doubles is one set to 5 points with a two-point lead, up to a maximum of 8.

9.3 If the two teams are playing on different table models, the first point is played on the first table. Then the table is changed and two balls are played on the second table. After that, two balls are again played on the first table, and so on. The winner of the coin toss chooses the starting table or the serve.

9.4 If both teams play on the same table model, the coin toss decides which team has the serve.

9.5 The side selection for the deciding doubles corresponds to the side selection of the match.

## 10. Referees

10.1 No referees are provided by the tournament directorate. However, it is permitted to resolve the question of refereeing bilaterally. If both parties have agreed on an unofficial referee, their decisions are valid and must be respected.

10.2 In the event of disagreement, the tournament directorate reserves the right to abandon the match and decide on the result and any sanctions.
