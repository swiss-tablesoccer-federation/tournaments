## 1. Introduction

This document regulates the financial matters of the sporting area of the Swiss Tablesoccer Federation, hereinafter referred to as STF.

The document is written in German. The German version is considered the original in word and writing. Versions in other languages are produced by electronic translation. Unless explicitly stated, all genders and age categories are meant.

### 1.1. Scope

The document Financial Regulations for Tournaments & Licensing is, from 01 January 2026, an integral part of the official federation documents and replaces all points which were previously covered on the topic of finances in the *Individual Sport Regulations*, the *Qualification ITSF World Cup Regulations* and the *Swiss Tablesoccer League Regulations*. All regulations and directives on the topic of finances within the aforementioned regulations become invalid upon the entry into force of this document.

### 1.2. Responsibility

The regulation is the responsibility of the Finance Department of the STF Board.

<finance@swisstablesoccer.ch>

The STF Board receives a right of veto for each amendment recommended for implementation by the Finance Department. This right of veto prevents the Finance Department from making amendments to the regulation that do not correspond to the strategic direction of the STF.

## 2. General Provisions

### 2.1. Data Protection

#### 2.1.1. Handling of Data

Persons who are active for the STF for the organization and execution of a tournament, the processing of registrations (online, offline), the management of participants' data, collection, participant recruitment and information, process the data on behalf of the STF and only for this purpose. According to law, they are obliged to monitor data processing by these persons and to oblige them not to process the data for their own purposes or to pass it on to third parties.

#### 2.1.2. Use of Data

The use of personal data is regulated in the applicable data protection declaration. The athletes undertake to make personal data of third parties available to the organizer only if they, or their legal representatives, are aware of the applicable data protection declaration and are entitled to do so under applicable data protection law. The organizer may at any time request that the athletes prove the existence of these prerequisites — in particular consent of the third parties — within a deadline, and may otherwise withdraw from all contracts without further ado.

### 2.2. General Requirements

#### 2.2.1. Personal Responsibility

Participation in STF events takes place at one's own risk and own responsibility. The tournament regulations also apply when registering other or additional persons (third parties). The organizer assumes that these third parties have given the registering person the authorization to register. When registering minor athletes through third parties, the organizer assumes that the consent of the legal representatives is in place.

#### 2.2.2. Liability

The organizer assumes no liability of any kind. This also applies to accidents, theft and liability towards third parties.

#### 2.2.3. Insurance

Insurance is the responsibility of the participants.

#### 2.2.4. Place of Jurisdiction

The place of jurisdiction is the location of the tournament organizer's headquarters.

#### 2.2.5. Swiss Olympics Ethics Charter of Swiss Sport

Appropriate behavior is expected of all participants at all times. The Swiss Olympics Ethics Charter of Swiss Sport applies. By participating in STF events, the Ethics Charter is tacitly accepted.

#### 2.2.6. Refunds

Anyone who does not compete in the tournament has no right to a refund of the entry fee, license costs or other expenses.

If a tournament is not held or is only partially held due to force majeure, there is no right to a refund of the entry fee.

## 3. Licensing

A license is required for all athletes to participate in the following STF events:

- Swiss Tablesoccer Series (STS)
- Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL)
- Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (CH Championship, official competitions)

The possible types of licensing are described in the following points.

### 3.1. License Types and Fees

| | **Competitive Sport (licensed)** | **Recreational Sport (unlic.)** | **Juniors (licensed)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual costs | ⚠️ Annual license, CHF 50<br>⚠️ Steering levy, CHF 30 | ✅ None | ⚠️ Annual license, CHF 20<br>✅ **No** steering levy |
| **Participation CH Championship** | ✅ Yes | ⛔ No | ✅ Yes |
| Participation STL | ✅ Yes | ⚠️ Tournament fee, CHF 15 | ✅ Yes |
| Participation STS *without ITSF status* | ✅ Yes | ⚠️ Tournament fee, CHF 15 | ✅ Yes |
| Participation STS *with ITSF status* | ✅ Yes | ⚠️ Tournament fee, CHF 15 | ✅ Yes |
| Participation STRT Pro | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| Participation STRT | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Participation ITSF Abroad** | ✅ Yes | ⛔ No | ✅ Yes |
| STF Ranking | ✅ Yes | ⛔ No | ✅ Yes |

*Table 1: Overview of License Types & Fees*

#### 3.1.1. Competitive Sport - Annual License

The annual license licenses players both nationally (STF) and internationally (ITSF). The annual license therefore enables participation in all open ITSF and STF tournaments, including the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (CH Championship) and the Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL).

The annual license for CHF 80 comes as a package and includes the fees and services listed in Table 1. Players can obtain an annual license through their club. The fees are invoiced to the club. Clubs are responsible for collecting the resulting license costs from their members. The license only becomes valid upon payment.

The annual license for juniors is analogous, but costs CHF 20.

#### 3.1.2. Recreational Sport - Tournament Fee

At STF events, players can participate in the current event by paying a tournament fee. The tournament fee entitles participation in the current event but not in the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (CH Championship). This means that qualification is only possible with a valid annual license. The general tournament license costs CHF 15 and is paid directly to the organizer. If the event additionally holds ITSF status (ITSF 250, ITSF 500, ITSF 750), then the necessary ITSF license for players from Switzerland (nationality or residence) is included in the tournament fee.

National athletes who only obtain an ITSF license to play abroad (e.g. Leonhart WS) will be invoiced the license costs for the ITSF license together with an administration fee of CHF 15.

#### 3.1.3. Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour (STRT & STRT Pro)

To participate in tournaments of the Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour (STRT & STRT Pro), <u>no</u> licensing is required. Instead, for each held, ranked tournament a fee of CHF 25 (STRT) or CHF 50 (STRT Pro) is due for the organizer. This flat rate will be invoiced by the STF directly to the organizing clubs after the end of the season. Organizing clubs are free to charge unlicensed players a tournament fee of a maximum of CHF 5.

#### 3.1.4. Administration Fee for Foreign Players

The administration fee for foreign players previously levied no longer applies. Foreign players instead pay the tournament fee (see Recreational Sport - Tournament Fee). For tournaments with ITSF status, an ITSF license is additionally required. Such a license can be obtained through their national federation. The status in Coral applies.

#### 3.1.5. Steering Levy for Refereeing

The introduction of a steering levy is intended to motivate the broad masses to take up referee training. The assumption is that, through the financial incentive, a large proportion of players will complete the basic training and thereby bring at least the theoretical requirements to be able to step in as a referee during tournaments.

The steering levy is graduated as follows:

| **Referee Status** | **Steering Levy** |
|---|---|
| Without training | CHF 30 |
| Assistant | CHF 10 |
| Regional or higher | CHF 0 |

The steering levy is part of the annual license and is invoiced together with it. For juniors, the steering levy does not apply regardless of referee status.

Information on training can be found at <https://referee.swisstablesoccer.ch>.

### 3.2. Application, Issuance & Billing

Licenses can be applied for at any time directly in Coral through a member club of the STF and by the players themselves, and are valid until the end of the calendar year. Licenses are released by the responsible person at the club.

The STF invoices the accruing license costs to its member clubs. Clubs are responsible for passing on the costs. Clubs are free to decide how to issue licenses and pass on the costs, e.g. the costs may be integrated into the membership fee or issued to guests with or without an additional administration fee.

A license that has been issued cannot be refunded; accruing costs must be paid.

## 4. Tournaments

### 4.1. Swiss Tablesoccer Finals

At the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (also Swiss Championship, CH Championship or SM), qualified athletes play for the respective title of official disciplines. The official disciplines of the CH Championship are defined in the Individual Sport Regulations.

#### 4.1.1. Entry Fee

No entry fee is charged for the official disciplines of the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals.

#### 4.1.2. Prizes

The winner receives a trophy and a medal. The teams in 2nd and 3rd place receive a medal. Prize money is also distributed from the SM pool.

#### 4.1.3. Prize Money Financing

At tournaments in the category *Swiss Tablesoccer Series*, 20% of the entry fee is diverted to the STF CH Championship. All payments from the disciplines Open Doubles, Open Singles, Women's Doubles, Women's Singles and Mixed are fully (100%) distributed at the CH Championship as follows.

For all disciplines of the Swiss Tablesoccer Series (STS) **except AD and AE**, entry fee is diverted to the organizer and to the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals. The key is as follows for all disciplines:

#### 4.2.4. Tournament Settlement

The organizer of an STS tournament is obliged to submit the settlement from Coral to the STF within one week after the event, so that the STF can issue an invoice for accruing costs such as transport flat rate, tournament fees, contributions to the SM pool, etc.

**Tournaments with ITSF status**

For tournaments in the STS category, the hosting club may apply for ITSF status. The costs and levels for this are set annually by the ITSF and adopted 1:1 by the STF. These costs are to be borne entirely by the club and will be invoiced by the STF.

### 4.3. Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour (STRT & STRT Pro)

#### 4.3.1. Entry Fee

The organizers of STRT tournaments may decide on the amount of the entry fees.

#### 4.3.2. Distribution of Entry Fees

The organizers of STRT tournaments may freely dispose of entry fees, i.e. pay them out in full, in part or not at all.

### 4.4. Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL)

#### 4.4.1. Entry Fee

The participation fee is CHF 150 per registered team. After the registration deadline for the Tablesoccer League, an invoice is sent to participating clubs, which must be paid within 20 days.

If a registered team does not compete, the participation fee will not be refunded.

#### 4.4.2. Distribution of Entry Fees

The entry fees go 80% to the STF and 20% to the organizing club, provided that the STF does not incur a loss through the STL.

#### 4.4.3. Payout Schedule

No prize money or entry fee is distributed.

### 4.5. ITSF World Cup Packages

Currently, no (travel) packages are defined for the ITSF World Cup (exception: juniors). Athletes who qualify for the ITSF World Cup through the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals are not entitled to financial support. If the STF's finances are stable, the Board may grant proportionate support measures, such as covering the entry fees.
