### 1. Introduction
  
This document regulates the financial matters of the sporting area of the Swiss Tablesoccer Federation, hereinafter STF.  
The document is written in German. The German version is deemed the original in word and writing. Versions in other languages are created by means of electronic translation. Unless explicitly stated otherwise, all genders and age categories are meant.

#### 1.1. Scope
  
The document Financial Regulations Tournament & Licensing System is an integral part of the official federation documents as of 01 January 2026 and replaces all points concerning finances that were previously reflected in the _Individual Sport_ Regulations, the _ITSF World Cup Qualification_ Regulations and the _Swiss Tablesoccer League_ Regulations. All rules and directives concerning finances within the aforementioned regulations become invalid upon entry into force of this document.

#### 1.2. Responsibility
  
The regulations fall under the responsibility of the Finance department of the STF Board.  
The STF Board has a veto right for every amendment recommended for implementation by the Finance department. This veto right prevents the Finance department from making amendments to the regulations that do not correspond to the strategic orientation of the STF.

### 2. General provisions

#### 2.1. Data protection

##### 2.1.1. Handling of data
  
Persons who work for the STF for the organization and staging of a tournament, the processing of registrations (online, offline), the management of participant data, debt collection, participant advertising and information process the data on behalf of the STF and solely for this purpose. By law, they are obliged to monitor data processing by these persons and to require them not to process the data for their own purposes or disclose it to third parties.

##### 2.1.2. Use of data
  
The use of personal data is governed by the applicable privacy policy. The athletes undertake to provide the organizer with personal data of third parties only if such persons, or their legal representatives, are aware of the applicable privacy policy and they are entitled to do so under applicable data protection law. The organizer may at any time request the athletes to prove, within a deadline, that these requirements have been met — in particular the consent of third parties — and may otherwise withdraw from all contracts without further notice.

#### 2.2. General requirements

##### 2.2.1. Personal responsibility
  
Participation in STF events is at one's own risk and on one's own responsibility. The tournament regulations also apply when registering other or additional persons (third parties). The organizer assumes that these third parties have authorized the registering person to register them. In the case of registration of minor athletes by third parties, the organizer assumes that the consent of the legal representatives has been obtained.

##### 2.2.2. Liability
  
The organizer does not accept liability of any kind. This also applies to accidents, theft and liability towards third parties.

##### 2.2.3. Insurance
  
Insurance is the responsibility of the participants.

##### 2.2.4. Place of jurisdiction
  
The place of jurisdiction is the registered office of the tournament organizer.

##### 2.2.5. Swiss Olympic Ethics Statute for Swiss Sport
  
Appropriate conduct is expected from all parties involved at all times. The Swiss Olympic Ethics Statute for Swiss Sport applies. By participating in STF events, the Ethics Statute is tacitly accepted.

##### 2.2.6. Refunds
  
Anyone who does not appear at the tournament has no right to reimbursement of the entry fee, licence costs or other expenses.  
If a tournament cannot be held or can only be partially held due to force majeure, there is no right to reimbursement of the entry fee.

- Swiss Tablesoccer Series (STS)
- Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL)
- Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (Swiss Championship, official events)

##### 3.1.1. Elite sport - annual licence
  
The annual licence licenses players both nationally (STF) and internationally (ITSF). The annual licence therefore enables participation in all open ITSF and STF tournaments, including Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (Swiss Championship) and Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL).  
The annual licence for CHF 80 comes as a package and includes the fees and services listed in Table 1. Players can obtain an annual licence through their club. The fees are invoiced to the club. Clubs are responsible for collecting the resulting licence costs from their members. The licence only becomes valid upon payment.  
The annual licence for juniors is analogous, but costs CHF 20.

##### 3.1.2. Grassroots sport - tournament fee
  
At STF events, players may participate in the current event against payment of a tournament fee. The tournament fee entitles participation in the current event but not in the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (Swiss Championship). This means qualification is only possible with a valid annual licence. The general tournament licence costs CHF 15 and is paid directly to the organizer. If the event additionally has ITSF status (ITSF 250, ITSF 500, ITSF 750), the required ITSF licence for players from Switzerland (nationality or residence) is included in the tournament fee.  
National athletes who only obtain an ITSF licence in order to play abroad (e.g. Leonhart WS) are invoiced for the ITSF licence costs together with an administration fee of CHF 15.

##### 3.1.3. Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour (STRT & STRT Pro)
  
No licence is required to participate in Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour tournaments (STRT & STRT Pro). Instead, a fee of CHF 25 (STRT) or CHF 50 (STRT Pro) is due from the organizer for each tournament held and counted. This flat fee is invoiced directly by the STF to the organizing clubs after the end of the season. Organizing clubs are free to charge non-licensed players a tournament fee of a maximum of CHF 5.

##### 3.1.4. Administration fee for foreign players
  
The administration fee previously charged for foreign players is discontinued. Foreign players instead pay the tournament fee (see Grassroots sport - tournament fee). For tournaments with ITSF status, an ITSF licence is also required. Such a licence can be obtained through their national federation. The status in Coral applies.

##### 3.1.5. Steering levy for refereeing
  
The introduction of a steering levy is intended to motivate the broad mass of players to take up referee training. The assumption is that the financial incentive will lead a large proportion of players to complete the basic training and thus have at least the theoretical requirements during tournaments to be able to step in as referees.

#### 3.2. Applying, granting & invoicing
  
Licences can be applied for at any time directly in Coral via an STF member club and by the players themselves and are valid until the end of the calendar year. Licences are approved by the person responsible at the club.  
The STF invoices the incurred licence costs to its member clubs. Clubs are responsible for passing on the costs. Clubs are free to decide how they issue licences and pass on the costs; for example, the costs may be integrated into the membership fee or issued to guests with or without an additional administration fee.  
A granted licence cannot be refunded; incurred costs must be paid.

### 4. Tournaments

#### 4.1. Swiss Tablesoccer Finals
  
At the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals (also Swiss Championship, CH Championship or SM), qualified athletes compete for the respective titles of official disciplines. The official disciplines of the Swiss Championship are defined in the Individual Sport Regulations.

##### 4.1.1. Entry fee
  
No entry fee is charged for the official disciplines of the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals.

##### 4.1.2. Prizes
  
The winner receives a trophy and a medal. The teams ranked 2nd and 3rd receive a medal. Prize money from the Swiss Championship pot is also paid out.

##### 4.1.3. Financing prize money
  
At tournaments in the _Swiss Tablesoccer Series_ category, 20% of the entry fee is diverted to the STF for the Swiss Championship. All payments from the disciplines Open Doubles, Open Singles, Woman Doubles, Woman Singles and Mixed are distributed 100% at the Swiss Championship as follows.

##### 4.2.4. Tournament settlement
  
The organizer of an STS tournament is obliged to submit the settlement from Coral to the STF within one week after the event so that the STF can issue an invoice for incurred costs such as transport flat rate, tournament fees, contributions to the Swiss Championship pot, etc.  
**Tournaments with ITSF status**  
For tournaments in the STS category, the hosting club may apply for ITSF status. The costs and levels for this are set annually by the ITSF and adopted 1:1 by the STF. These costs are to be borne entirely by the club and are invoiced by the STF.

#### 4.3. Swiss Tablesoccer Regio Tour (STRT & STRT Pro)

##### 4.3.1. Entry fee
  
The organizers of STRT tournaments may decide the amount of the entry fees.

##### 4.3.2. Allocation of entry fees
  
The organizers of STRT tournaments may freely dispose of entry fees, i.e. pay them out in full, in part or not at all.

#### 4.4. Swiss Tablesoccer League (STL)

##### 4.4.1. Entry fee
  
The participation fee is CHF 150 per registered team. After the registration deadline for the Tablesoccer League, an invoice is sent to participating clubs, which must be paid within 20 days.  
If a registered team does not appear, the participation fee will not be refunded.

##### 4.4.2. Allocation of entry fees
  
80% of the entry fees go to the STF and 20% to the organizing club, provided that the STF does not incur a loss through the STL.

##### 4.4.3. Payout key
  
No prize money or entry fee is paid out.

#### 4.5. ITSF World Cup packages
  
Currently, no (travel) packages for the ITSF World Cup are defined (exception: juniors). Athletes who qualify for the ITSF World Cup through the Swiss Tablesoccer Finals have no entitlement to financial support. If the finances of the STF are stable, the Board may grant proportionate support, such as covering entry fees.
