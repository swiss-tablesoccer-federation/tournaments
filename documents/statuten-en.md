## I - General provisions

### A Fundamental principles

#### Article 1: Name and registered office

1. The Swiss Tablesoccer Federation (STF) is an association within the meaning of Articles 60 et seq. of the Swiss Civil Code.
2. The registered office of the STF is in Ittigen near Bern.

#### Article 2: Purpose

1. The STF and its members aim to develop and promote table football in Switzerland as both elite and grassroots sport, in particular by organizing, arranging and holding national matches in accordance with national and international regulations. As a sports organization, the STF represents the interests of Swiss table football at international and national level together with its members.
2. asdfThe federation aims to protect the integrity, safety and fair play of table football sports competitions against any form of manipulation and/or corrupt activity.
3. The STF is active throughout Switzerland and represents all Swiss regions.
4. The STF provides services to its members.
5. The STF issues the licences required to participate in official tournaments. The regulations governing the issuing of licences set out the details.
6. The STF may carry out any other activity directly or indirectly related to its purpose. The STF may perform all acts that are directly or indirectly useful to its purpose. To achieve its purpose, it may establish companies, acquire interests in them and sell interests.

#### Article 3: Memberships

1. The FTS is a member of Swiss Olympic, the umbrella organization of Swiss sport.
2. The STF is a member of the International Table Soccer Federation (ITSF). In addition, the STF may join any other association whose purpose is closely connected with table football.
3. In addition to these statutes and regulations, the STF is subject to the statutes, regulations and current rules of play of the ITSF.

#### Article 4: Ethics and doping

1. The STF is committed to healthy, respectful, fair and successful sport. The federation—as well as its bodies and members—sets an example of these values by respecting others and by acting and communicating transparently. The STF recognizes the current “Ethics Charter” of Swiss sport and passes on its principles to its member clubs.
2. The STF, its direct and indirect member organizations and all persons mentioned on page 4 (“Personal scope of application”) of the Swiss Olympic Doping Statute (“Doping Statute”) or in Article 1 of the Ethics Statute for Swiss Sport (“Ethics Statute”) are subject to the Doping Statute or the Ethics Statute. The STF ensures that all these persons, insofar as they belong to or can be attributed to the STF, acknowledge and comply with the Doping Statute and the Ethics Statute.
3. Suspected violations of the Doping Statute or the Ethics Statute are investigated by Swiss Sport Integrity and may be sanctioned in accordance with the cases defined in the Ethics Statute.
4. The Swiss Sports Tribunal has exclusive jurisdiction, as the first instance, for the legal classification and sanctioning of violations of the Doping Statute. It applies its procedural rules.
5. Decisions of the Swiss Sports Tribunal may be appealed to the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) in Lausanne, to the exclusion of state courts, within 21 days of receipt of the reasoned decision.

#### Article 5: Dissolution

1. The Delegates’ Assembly may decide to dissolve the STF at an extraordinary assembly convened for this purpose.
2. If the STF is dissolved, the Delegates’ Assembly appoints the liquidators and grants signing authority in the event of liquidation.
3. The Delegates’ Assembly remains fully competent during liquidation.
4. In the event of liquidation, the Delegates’ Assembly disposes of the remaining assets after settlement of any debts, unless the law provides otherwise.
5. A merger may only take place with another legal entity domiciled in Switzerland and exempt from tax charges because of its non-profit or charitable purpose. In the event of dissolution, profits and capital shall be transferred to another legal entity domiciled in Switzerland and exempt from tax charges because of a non-profit or charitable purpose. Any distribution of the association’s assets among the members is excluded. This rule is irrevocable.

#### Article 6: Recusal

1. Members of the bodies perform their duties in the interest of the STF. They organize their personal and professional relationships so as to avoid any conflict of interest with the STF. Persons whose interests are in permanent conflict with the interests of the STF may not belong to any of the STF’s bodies.
2. All members of the STF’s bodies must immediately disclose any conflict of interest to the chair of the board. In the event of a conflict of interest affecting the chair of the board, the chair shall inform all members of the board.
3. A conflict of interest exists in particular if a member:
  - either personally or as a body of a legal entity, has an interest in the outcome of the decision of the STF’s judicial bodies;
  - could be biased for any other reason, namely if there is a relationship of friendship, hostility or dependence between them and one of the parties affected by a decision of the STF’s judicial bodies;
  - is married, related by marriage or related in a direct line or collateral line up to the third degree to one of the parties or their representative.
4. In the event of a conflict of interest, members of the bodies recuse themselves and are excluded from voting rights.
5. If the STF regulations do not provide for the modalities of recusal and decision-making authority, the STF board shall decide on the admissibility of the recusal. The intensity of the conflict of interest must be taken into account.

#### Article 7: Liability

  
The debts of the STF are settled exclusively from the association’s assets.

#### Article 8: Official languages

1. The official languages of the STF are German, French and Italian.
2. All essential documents are drafted in German and French and must be kept up to date. Where possible, fundamental documents must also be translated into Italian.
3. In certain cases, English may be considered the fourth official language if all language regions agree.
4. Everyone may express themselves and submit motions in one of the official languages. Everyone may request to receive essential documents in the desired official language.
5. In the event of any contradiction between texts drafted in the official languages, the German text prevails.

#### Article 9: Information, publication and transparency

  
The STF is obliged to inform its members, the media and the public. The board regulates the details of information, publications and transparency.

### B Members

#### Article 10: Clubs

1. All clubs established as legal entities, for example in the form of a stock corporation or association, may become members of the STF.
2. Members act autonomously within the limits of their statutes; these may not contain any provision contrary to the rules of the STF. By joining the STF, members and their associates or members expressly recognize the primacy of STF law.
3. All clubs must maintain secrecy regarding all affairs of the STF and the clubs. This also applies to information that a club receives from the STF or from other clubs outside the STF.

#### Article 11: Admission

1. To join the STF, a written application for admission must be sent to the STF board. A signed copy of the applicant’s statutes must be submitted, as well as a signed declaration in which the applicant affirms that it and all its members are subject to and accept the statutes and regulations of the STF.
2. The board decides on membership at its next meeting. The applicant will be informed in writing of the board’s decision.

#### Article 12: Rights and obligations

1. Members are required to safeguard the interests of the STF, comply with the statutes and regulations of the STF and observe the instructions and decisions of the STF’s bodies.
2. Clubs undertake to respect each other, even in exercising their own rights and pursuing their own interests, and to safeguard the well-being of the STF and the clubs in their activities. For its part, the STF continuously analyses how its decisions affect the clubs.
3. Federation members are required to refrain from any form of undue influence and manipulation of sports competitions and to comply in particular with the corresponding provisions of the Swiss Olympic Ethics Statutes.
4. The STF is not liable for accidents, property damage or civil liability cases caused by members, their bodies, officials, referees, coaches and players while carrying out their activities. Members are themselves responsible for risk management, including taking out appropriate insurance.

#### Article 13: Termination of membership

1. Members may terminate their membership of the STF by giving three months’ notice to the end of the current financial year (see Article 35). Termination is effected by registered letter addressed to the STF board.
2. Membership lapses immediately in the event of bankruptcy or dissolution of a member.
3. After being heard, members acting contrary to the interests of the STF or seriously damaging its honour may be expelled from the STF by the STF board and, on the latter’s recommendation, by a decision of the Delegates’ Assembly.

#### Article 14: Honorary member

  
The board may submit to the vote of the Delegates’ Assembly the admission, as honorary members, of persons who have rendered exceptional service to the STF or have particularly distinguished themselves through personal commitment to the purpose of the association.

## II Organization

### A Delegates’ Assembly

#### Article 15: General provisions

  
The ordinary Delegates’ Assembly of the STF meets once a year (Art. 18, paragraph 2). Members or their delegates present at the assembly must have the opportunity to ask questions about all areas of activity of the STF, make suggestions and engage in discussions. The delegates represent the interests of the clubs.

#### Article 16: Composition

1. The Delegates’ Assembly is composed of the delegates sent by the member clubs.
2. The Delegates’ Assembly is chaired by the president of the STF or, if the president is prevented from attending, by the vice-president of the STF. The president conducts the Delegates’ Assembly in a balanced and purposeful manner. The president thereby ensures that members can exercise their rights.

#### Article 17: Duties and powers

1. The Delegates’ Assembly is the supreme body of the STF. Its rights and obligations arise from the law and the statutes. The Delegates’ Assembly has the following non-transferable powers:
  - Determination of framework conditions:
    - determination and amendment of the statutes and philosophy;
    - decision on the dissolution, transformation and any other exceptional strategic and/or structural decision of the STF;
  - Personal decisions:
    - exclusion of members
    - election and removal of board members
    - election and removal of the president or co-presidents of the STF
    - election and removal of the auditing body (if required by law)
    - election and removal of honorary members;
  - Other decisions:
    - approval of the annual report and annual accounts
    - decision on the allocation of results
    - decision on granting discharge to the board
    - acknowledgment of strategic objectives and the budget
    - decision on matters reserved to the Delegates’ Assembly by law, the statutes or the regulations of the STF, or submitted to it by the board.
2. The function of the Delegates’ Assembly is to define and respect the collective will of the members.

#### Article 18: Voting rights

1. Member clubs send one to a maximum of three delegates to the Delegates’ Assembly; however, each club has only one vote.
2. Member clubs may be represented at the Delegates’ Assembly by any of their members.
3. Clubs may not transfer their vote to other clubs.

#### Article 19: Notice and agenda

1. The Delegates’ Assembly is convened by the chair of the STF board and, if the chair is prevented from attending, by the vice-chair.
2. The ordinary Delegates’ Assembly meets each year, no later than six months after the end of the financial year.
3. Extraordinary Delegates’ Assemblies are convened as required, in particular if one fifth of the members, three board members, the auditing body or the liquidators of the STF request this in writing, stating the reasons and proposals. Extraordinary Delegates’ Assemblies must meet within 40 days of receipt of the request. Members may demand the convening of such an assembly by legal action.
4. Notice of the Delegates’ Assembly is sent in writing (by post or e-mail) to the club president at least 20 days before the date of the assembly. The notice must state the agenda items as well as the proposals of the board and of the persons who requested the holding of a Delegates’ Assembly or the consideration of an agenda item. With the notice of the ordinary Delegates’ Assembly, the management report (annual accounts, annual report) and the audit report, as well as the statutes in the event of a revision of the statutes, must be sent to members entitled to vote at the Delegates’ Assembly.

#### Article 20: Voting

1. Any Delegates’ Assembly convened in accordance with the statutes may validly deliberate regardless of the number of members present.
2. The Delegates’ Assembly takes its decisions and conducts elections by a simple majority of the votes cast, unless the law or the statutes provide otherwise. Abstentions are not taken into account when calculating the simple majority.
3. In the event of a tie, the president has the casting vote. **Example of simple majority:** A motion is deemed accepted if it receives more votes in favour than against; abstentions are not taken into account. A three-quarters majority of the votes represented, with at least two-thirds of the votes of the Delegates’ Assembly present, is required for:
  - any amendment to the statutes
  - the removal of a person appointed by the Delegates’ Assembly before the end of their term of office
  - the dissolution of the STF
4. Each delegate has one vote.

#### Article 21: Minutes

1. The president appoints the secretary who will draw up the minutes of the decisions and votes. The minutes must be signed by the president and the secretary.
2. The minutes of the Delegates’ Assembly are communicated to the delegates and board members 10 working days after the Delegates’ Assembly (by post or e-mail).

#### Article 22: Information and inspection

1. During the Delegates’ Assembly, each delegate may request from the board any information relating to the affairs of the STF and may request from the auditing body any information relating to the organization and result of its audit.
2. The board may only refuse to provide information that could harm business secrets or other legitimate interests of the STF.
3. The accounting books and correspondence may only be inspected with the express authorization of the Delegates’ Assembly or by decision of the board, while preserving business secrets.

### B Board

#### Article 23: General provisions

  
The board meets as often as business requires, generally four times a year.

#### Article 24: Composition

1. The board consists of at least seven and at most thirteen members. It is elected by the Delegates’ Assembly.
2. The board constitutes itself.
3. The term of office of board members is two years. Repeated nomination of a representative or re-election is possible. The maximum term of office is 12 years. Interrupted mandates are not taken into account.
4. Board members may resign at any time without stating reasons.
5. The composition of the board is balanced and broadly based. Both sexes must be represented by at least 40% each. In addition, the committee must include at least one athlete. Each board member is autonomous in critical exchanges. The members of the STF board have the necessary sporting, legal, economic and social skills. Board members must be available and identify with the objectives of the STF.
6. The board is led by the president and, if the president is prevented from attending, by the vice-president. The president leads the board in a balanced and purposeful manner.

#### Article 25: Duties and powers

1. The board is responsible for the overall management and supervision of the STF. It represents the STF externally and may take decisions in all areas that are not assigned to another body by law, the statutes or the regulations.
2. The board determines the strategy and general means for achieving the strategic objectives. The board performs the following non-transferable duties:
- exercising the overall management of the STF and issuing the necessary instructions;
- implementing the decisions of the Delegates’ Assembly;
- admitting members to the STF;
- determining the organization and establishing internal regulations or any other regulations whose establishment is not expressly reserved to another body;
- establishing financial regulations;
- defining the principles of accounting, financial control, financial planning and risk management;
- selecting the head coach of the national teams (women / men / juniors / seniors / wheelchair users);
- preparing a management report (annual accounts, annual report and, where applicable, consolidated accounts), as well as preparing the Delegates’ Assembly;
- regularly providing members and other stakeholders with information on table football topics;
- mediating between the elite sport sector on the one hand and youth development and amateur sport on the other;
- prior approval of all matters of board members concerning table football and not falling within these persons’ main activities within the STF. This applies in particular to competing activities and to obtaining benefits or compensation for themselves or relatives;
- analyzing the performance of the board and holding an annual performance review with its members;
- the board may, at any time and ex officio, make purely editorial changes to these statutes and to all regulations.
- The board of directors’ spending authority is limited to a maximum of CHF 10,000 in individual cases.

#### Article 26: Voting rights
  
Each board member participating in a board meeting has one vote.

#### Article 27: Notice and agenda

1. The board meets at the invitation of the president or of the vice-president designated by the president if the president is prevented from attending.
2. Except in urgent cases, notices are sent in writing (by post or e-mail) at least 10 days before the meeting, indicating the agenda items and motions.
3. Each board member may request the president in writing, 20 days before the meeting, to place an item on the agenda, stating their motion.
4. Furthermore, each board member may request in writing that the board be convened, stating the reason. In this case, the notice must be issued within a reasonable time.
5. For essential matters, the board may involve external experts. Board members participate in the meetings. Board meetings may also be held by telephone.

#### Article 28: Voting

1. The board takes its decisions and conducts elections by an absolute majority of the votes cast, unless the law or the statutes provide otherwise. Abstentions are not taken into account when calculating the absolute majority. The quorum is reached only if at least four members are present or participate in a written vote.
2. Votes, elections and nominations for votes by the board are by open ballot, unless at least one board member requests a secret ballot.
3. A written vote among board members is equivalent to a board decision, unless a board member requests an oral or telephone deliberation. Decisions taken in writing must be recorded in the minutes of the next board meeting.

#### Article 29: Minutes

1. The president appoints the secretary, who is not required to be a board member. The secretary records the decisions and votes. The minutes must be signed by the president and the secretary.
2. The minutes of board meetings are communicated to the delegates and board members 20 working days after the meeting (by post or e-mail).

#### Article 30: Information and inspection

1. During and outside meetings, each board member may request each other board member to be informed, without restriction, on all matters concerning the STF and to be able to inspect all books, files and documents of the STF. All board members are required to provide the information and present the requested documents without restriction.
2. If a board member exercises their right to information and inspection abusively or in a manner that disrupts the regular conduct of business, the board may decide to grant that member a restricted right to information, by analogy with the rule provided for in Art. 715a CO.

#### Article 31: Compensation

1. In principle, each board member performs their duties voluntarily and free of charge. Expenses incurred by board members as a result of their activity on the board shall be reimbursed up to the amounts actually incurred. The amount of compensation is determined by separate regulations.

#### Article 32: Reimbursement of benefits

1. Board members and their relatives who benefit from or receive benefits from third parties because of their activity within the association must reimburse them if these benefits are not reported and authorized in advance by the board.
2. The reimbursement obligation becomes time-barred five years after receipt of the benefit.

### C Auditing body

#### Article 33: Composition

1. The members’ assembly elects (cf. Article 17), for a two-year term of office, two natural persons as auditors of the accounts. Re-election is permitted.
2. The audit may also be entrusted to a single legal entity (for example a fiduciary company, etc.).

#### Article 34: Duties and powers

1. The auditing body is responsible for verifying the accuracy of the annual accounts. It is entitled at any time to inspect the accounts and supporting documents.
2. The auditing body prepares a written report for the members’ assembly.

## III Finances

#### Article 35: Fundamental principles

1. The STF does not pursue any profit-making purpose. It is nevertheless required to maintain sound finances by creating sufficient reserves.
2. Income is distributed according to objective criteria based on the clubs’ contributions to the STF. The STF designs precise guidelines for the allocation of its income. The management and distribution of income are transparent and responsible.
3. The STF’s accounting is carried out in accordance with the statutory standards (CO). Compliance with the rules on accounting, the balance sheet, the income statement, the allocation of profits and reserves in accordance with Articles 662 et seq. and Articles 957 et seq. CO is guaranteed.
4. The board has individual signing authority up to an amount of CHF 2,500 and collective signing authority for higher amounts. Each investment must be authorized in advance by the board.<br>Individual projects involving expenditure of more than CHF 10,000 must always be presented to the delegates before they are initiated. Resolutions may be adopted either at the Delegates’ Assembly or electronically. Resolutions are adopted in accordance with Art. 20, para. 2 above.
5. The board establishes financial regulations.

#### Article 36: Membership fees

1. The amount of membership fees is set each year by the Delegates’ Assembly.
2. Each STF member club pays an annual membership fee to the STF.
3. The total amount of the membership fee depends on the date on which the member joins or leaves during the financial year.

#### Article 37: Other income

  
The other sources of income necessary to pursue the association’s purpose are:
1. player licences
2. income from organized events
3. sponsorship income
4. subsidies
5. income from service contracts
6. donations and support of any kind

#### Article 38: Financial year

  
The financial year runs from January 1 to December 31.
